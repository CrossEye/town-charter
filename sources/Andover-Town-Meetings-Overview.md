Andover Town Meetings: When Are They Required and How Do They Work?
===================================================================

*[← Back to Index](Index.md)*

This document explains the three types of town meeting in Andover, Connecticut — what triggers each, who can call them, what they can and cannot decide, and what happens when things go wrong. References to Charter sections link to the online version at [charter2024.andoverct.info](https://charter2024.andoverct.info). State statutes link to the Connecticut General Assembly's published text.

----------



The Town Meeting as Legislative Body
--------------------------------------

Under [Section 301](https://charter2024.andoverct.info/#Section301), the Town Meeting is Andover's **legislative body**. It has final authority over the matters specifically enumerated in the Charter and legislative authority for all other matters not reserved to the Board of Selectmen under [Section 402](https://charter2024.andoverct.info/#Section402). All persons eligible to vote in Town Meetings under Connecticut law may vote, and the same electors may vote in referenda called pursuant to [Sections 303](https://charter2024.andoverct.info/#Section303), [304](https://charter2024.andoverct.info/#Section304), and [305](https://charter2024.andoverct.info/#Section305).

----------



Three Types of Town Meeting
-----------------------------

The Charter establishes three distinct types of town meeting, each with its own trigger, subject matter, and procedural rules:

1. **The Annual Town Budget Meeting** — held in the second week of April each year, for the sole purpose of acting on the proposed budget ([Section 303](https://charter2024.andoverct.info/#Section303)).

2. **Special Town Meetings** — called by the Board of Selectmen as needed for specific categories of business enumerated in [Section 304](https://charter2024.andoverct.info/#Section304).

3. **Petitioned Town Meetings** — called by the Board of Selectmen in response to a petition signed by at least 2% of the registered electors, on specific subjects enumerated in [Section 305](https://charter2024.andoverct.info/#Section305).

----------



General Procedures Applicable to All Town Meetings
-----------------------------------------------------


### Calling and Notice ###

All Town Meetings must be called by resolution of the Board of Selectmen under [C.G.S. 7-3](https://www.cga.ct.gov/current/pub/chap_090.htm#sec_7-3), which fixes the time and place ([Section 302A](https://charter2024.andoverct.info/#Section302A)). Notice must be given at least **five days in advance** by:

- Publication in a newspaper of general circulation in the Town;
- Posting in a public meeting place and on the Town Office Public Notice Bulletin Board; and
- **US mail to all eligible voters**, including the date, location, and agenda.

If the meeting will send a matter to referendum, the mailed notice must also include the date, time, and location of the referendum, whether absentee voting is permitted, and how to obtain an absentee ballot.


### Quorum ###

A quorum of **at least 25 electors** is required to conduct business ([Section 302B](https://charter2024.andoverct.info/#Section302B)). The Town Clerk (or a designated alternate) uses a checklist to verify the eligibility of attendees. The First Selectman or designee calls the meeting to order and immediately ascertains whether a quorum is present.

**If no quorum is present**, the First Selectman recesses the meeting to the same day of the following week. If there is still no quorum at the second meeting, all matters on the call **revert to the Board of Selectmen**, who shall have final authority — *except* for the budget, which proceeds under [Section 803](https://charter2024.andoverct.info/#Section803) (see the [Andover Budget Process overview](Andover-Budget-Process-Overview.md) for details).


### The Moderator ###

The first order of business at every Town Meeting is the election of a moderator. The moderator must be an elector of the Town and must have a working knowledge of Robert's Rules of Order ([Section 306A](https://charter2024.andoverct.info/#Section306A)). All substantive business is conducted under the moderator's leadership. Any Town Meeting may be recessed from time to time as the interests of the Town require.


### Voting ###

All actions at Town Meetings are decided by **majority vote of those present and voting**, provided a quorum is present ([Section 302B](https://charter2024.andoverct.info/#Section302B)).

- **Budget votes** are always by paper ballot.
- **All other votes** are by paper ballot if at least 20% of those present and voting request it; otherwise, voting may proceed in any orderly manner the moderator directs.

----------



The Annual Town Budget Meeting
--------------------------------

The Annual Town Budget Meeting is held in the **second week of April** each year ([Section 303](https://charter2024.andoverct.info/#Section303)). The proposed budget is the only substantive matter on the agenda. General town-meeting procedures under [C.G.S. 7-7](https://www.cga.ct.gov/current/pub/chap_090.htm#sec_7-7) are explicitly excluded from this meeting by [Section 803A](https://charter2024.andoverct.info/#Section803A).

What the Town Meeting can and cannot do with the budget is covered in detail in the [Andover Budget Process overview](Andover-Budget-Process-Overview.md). In brief: it may reduce but not increase the proposed budget, may not alter any specific line items in the Board of Education's budget, and adjourns to a referendum rather than taking a final vote itself.

**If no quorum is achieved at the Annual Town Budget Meeting** (or at the recessed second meeting), the budget goes directly to referendum without any opportunity for amendment ([Section 803C](https://charter2024.andoverct.info/#Section803C)).

----------



Special Town Meetings: What Triggers Them
------------------------------------------

[Section 304](https://charter2024.andoverct.info/#Section304) lists the categories of business that **must** be submitted to a Special Town Meeting — provided all other prerequisites have been met first. The full list:

**A. Bonds and Long-Term Financing ([Section 304A](https://charter2024.andoverct.info/#Section304A))**
The issuance of bonds or any form of financing with a term exceeding one year. See [Section 810](https://charter2024.andoverct.info/#Section810) and the [Spending Beyond the Annual Budget overview](Andover-Spending-Beyond-Budget-Overview.md) for full detail on borrowing thresholds.

**B. Large Supplemental Appropriations ([Section 304B](https://charter2024.andoverct.info/#Section304B))**
Supplemental appropriations exceeding the Board of Finance's 0.5% cumulative limit under [Section 805B](https://charter2024.andoverct.info/#Section805B). If the supplemental appropriation equals or exceeds 2.5% of the current year budget (excluding the Region 8 appropriation), it bypasses the Town Meeting entirely and goes directly to referendum. See the [Spending Beyond the Annual Budget overview](Andover-Spending-Beyond-Budget-Overview.md) for more.

**C. Real Estate ([Section 304C](https://charter2024.andoverct.info/#Section304C))**
Any acquisition or disposition of fee title to real estate by the Town.

**D. Permanent Boards and Agencies ([Section 304D](https://charter2024.andoverct.info/#Section304D))**
The creation, consolidation, modification, or abolition of any permanent board, commission, department, or agency not otherwise provided for in the Charter.

**E. Multi-Year Leases ([Section 304E](https://charter2024.andoverct.info/#Section304E))**
Leases or lease options to which the Town (including the local Board of Education) is a party, with a term or obligation exceeding one year — excluding leases of personal property.

**F. Capital Reserve Fund Draws ([Section 304F](https://charter2024.andoverct.info/#Section304F))**
Any appropriation from the Capital and Non-Recurring Expense Fund (not included in the annual budget) exceeding 0.25% of the current budget.

**G. Large Grant Programs ([Section 304G](https://charter2024.andoverct.info/#Section304G))**
Participation in any federal, state, or private grant program worth more than $2,500 (excluding Boards of Education) if the Town's required contribution exceeds $25,000.

**H. Road Discontinuances ([Section 304H](https://charter2024.andoverct.info/#Section304H))**
The discontinuance or abandonment of Town roads.

**I. Selectmen's Discretion ([Section 304I](https://charter2024.andoverct.info/#Section304I))**
Any other matter the Board of Selectmen deems of sufficient importance to submit to a Special Town Meeting, including ordinance adoptions, modifications, or repeals.

**J. Petitioned Ordinances ([Section 304J](https://charter2024.andoverct.info/#Section304J))**
The approval, repeal, or modification of any Town ordinance when petitioned under [Section 305](https://charter2024.andoverct.info/#Section305).

> **A note on ordinances**: The Board of Selectmen may enact *minor ordinances* on its own, without a Town Meeting. "Minor ordinances" are defined in [Section 105H](https://charter2024.andoverct.info/#Section105H) as those necessary for the orderly day-to-day maintenance of the Town, such as setting speed limits on Town roads or fixing hours for Town facilities and offices. *Major* ordinances — those that are not minor, or that the Board chooses to submit — require a public hearing and the process described in [Section 404](https://charter2024.andoverct.info/#Section404), which may include a Special Town Meeting.

----------



Petitioned Town Meetings: When Residents Can Force a Meeting
-------------------------------------------------------------

[Section 305](https://charter2024.andoverct.info/#Section305) gives Andover residents a direct-democracy mechanism: any group of electors equaling at least **2% of the registered voters** (based on the most recent official registry lists) may petition for a Town Meeting on certain subjects.

The subjects available to a Petitioned Town Meeting are a subset of the Special Town Meeting list:

- [Section 304C](https://charter2024.andoverct.info/#Section304C): Real estate (except acquisitions by purchase, which cannot be compelled by petition)
- [Section 304D](https://charter2024.andoverct.info/#Section304D): Permanent boards and agencies
- [Section 304H](https://charter2024.andoverct.info/#Section304H): Road discontinuances
- [Section 304J](https://charter2024.andoverct.info/#Section304J): Petitioned ordinances

Note that residents cannot petition for a Town Meeting on bonds, supplemental appropriations, multi-year leases, capital reserve draws, or grant programs — only the Board of Selectmen can initiate those.


### The Petition Process ###

Petitions are submitted on a form provided by the Town Clerk. Before the meeting, the proposal is reviewed by the **Town Attorney**, who may correct it for repetitions, illegalities, and unconstitutional provisions — but may not materially change its meaning or effect ([Section 305A](https://charter2024.andoverct.info/#Section305A)).

Within **seven days** of receiving the petitions, the Town Clerk reports to the Selectmen whether sufficient valid signatures were obtained. If signatures are insufficient, the Clerk notifies the Selectmen, who declare the petition invalid. If sufficient, the Clerk certifies the count and the Selectmen must call a Petitioned Town Meeting within **30 days** ([Section 305B](https://charter2024.andoverct.info/#Section305B)).

Once called, a Petitioned Town Meeting follows the same procedures as a Special Town Meeting. This right to petition is in addition to, and does not replace, any rights electors may have to petition for a Special Town Meeting under Connecticut General Statutes.

----------



What the Town Meeting Decides vs. What It Does Not
----------------------------------------------------

It is worth being explicit about the limits of Town Meeting authority:

**The Town Meeting decides:**
- Whether to approve (or reduce) the proposed annual budget, subject to referendum
- Whether to authorize bond issuance (for bonds under 10% of the tax levy)
- Whether to approve large supplemental appropriations (0.5%–2.5% range)
- Whether to approve or reject real estate transactions, multi-year leases, major grant programs, road discontinuances, and changes to permanent boards
- Whether to adopt, modify, or repeal major ordinances

**The Town Meeting does not decide:**
- The content or line items of the Board of Education's budget (it may reduce the total, but may not alter individual line items)
- Whether to *increase* the proposed budget beyond what the Board of Finance submitted (it cannot)
- Administrative appointments (those belong to the Board of Selectmen or the affected boards)
- Minor ordinances (those belong to the Board of Selectmen)
- Emergency appropriations up to $10,000 (those belong to the Board of Selectmen)
- Day-to-day operations of any Town department or agency

**On most matters, the referendum makes the final decision** — the Town Meeting acts, and the question then goes to the voters. The exception is ordinances and most Special Town Meeting business, where Town Meeting approval is final (no automatic referendum follows). The budget, however, always proceeds to referendum regardless of what happens at Town Meeting.

Summary: Which Meeting for Which Subject?
------------------------------------------

| Subject                                 | Who Can Call                | Meeting Type                   | Goes to Referendum? |
| --------------------------------------- | --------------------------- | ------------------------------ | ------------------- |
| Annual budget                           | Board of Finance (via BOS)  | Annual Town Budget Meeting      | Yes (always)        |
| Bonds / long-term financing             | Board of Selectmen          | Special Town Meeting            | Yes (if >10% levy, directly) |
| Supplemental appropriation, 0–0.5%     | Board of Finance            | None required                  | No                  |
| Supplemental appropriation, 0.5%–2.5%  | Board of Selectmen          | Special Town Meeting            | Yes                 |
| Supplemental appropriation, ≥2.5%      | Board of Finance            | None (goes directly to ref.)   | Yes                 |
| Real estate acquisition/disposition     | Board of Selectmen or 2%    | Special or Petitioned TM       | No (unless bonds)   |
| Multi-year leases                       | Board of Selectmen          | Special Town Meeting            | No                  |
| Large grant programs (>$25K match)      | Board of Selectmen          | Special Town Meeting            | No                  |
| Capital reserve draw, >0.25% of budget | Board of Selectmen          | Special Town Meeting            | No                  |
| Road discontinuance                     | Board of Selectmen or 2%    | Special or Petitioned TM       | No                  |
| Major ordinances                        | Board of Selectmen or 2%    | Special or Petitioned TM       | No                  |
| Create/modify/abolish boards            | Board of Selectmen or 2%    | Special or Petitioned TM       | No                  |

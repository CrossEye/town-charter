Suggested Charter Revisions: BOF Authority Over the BOE Budget

Proposed language changes to resolve the ambiguity in Sections 802E and 802F while preserving the BOE's independence over its internal spending.

The Problem

The current Charter uses the phrase "only comment and make recommendations" in Section 802E to describe the BOF's authority over the BOE budget. This language is ambiguous: it can be read as prohibiting the BOF from changing the BOE's bottom-line number, or as limiting the BOF to bottom-line adjustments without line-item control. The ambiguity has produced competing legal opinions and ongoing confusion among board members and residents.

The goal of these suggestions is to make the Charter say clearly what (under the structural analysis) it likely already means:

  • The BOF can adjust the BOE's total appropriation as part of preparing the final budget.
  • The BOF cannot direct how the BOE spends its appropriation internally.
  • The BOE retains its independence over line-item allocation, consistent with C.G.S. 10-222.

Suggestion 1: Revise Section 802E (Primary Fix)

Current Language

In preparing the proposed budget, the Board of Finance may add to, delete from, or eliminate requests made by the various departments, offices, and agencies except that the Board may only comment and make recommendations on the budget requests of the local Board of Education.

Proposed Language

In preparing the proposed budget, the Board of Finance may add to, delete from, or eliminate requests made by the various departments, offices, and agencies. With respect to the local Board of Education, the Board of Finance may adjust the total amount to be appropriated, but shall not add to, delete from, or alter specific line items within the Board of Education budget. The Board of Education shall retain sole discretion over the allocation of its appropriated funds among line items, consistent with Section 10-222 of the Connecticut General Statutes.

What This Does

  • Replaces the ambiguous "only comment and make recommendations" with explicit language granting bottom-line authority and denying line-item authority.
  • Names the C.G.S. 10-222 principle directly, so readers don't have to infer it.
  • Preserves the BOE's meaningful independence — it controls how to spend its money — while making clear that the BOF controls how much.

Suggestion 2: Revise Section 802F (Clarifying Companion)

Current Language

Subsequent to such hearing and review, the Board of Finance shall prepare a final budget that incorporates any recommended changes to be presented to the Annual Town Budget Meeting, which shall be in the same format and contain the same information as is provided for in Section 802E(1-5) above.

Proposed Language

Subsequent to such hearing and review, the Board of Finance shall prepare a final budget to be presented to the Annual Town Budget Meeting. The final budget shall reflect the Board of Finance's determinations on appropriation amounts for all departments, offices, agencies, and the local Board of Education, and shall be in the same format and contain the same information as is provided for in Section 802E(1-5) above.

What This Does

  • Removes "recommended changes," which is the other source of ambiguity (are the "recommendations" binding or advisory?).
  • Makes clear that the final budget reflects the BOF's determinations, not merely its suggestions.
  • Applies uniformly to all budget components, including the BOE.

Suggestion 3: Add a New Section 802G (Optional — Belt and Suspenders)

If the revision commission wants to be maximally clear and head off future disputes, a new subsection could spell out the distinct roles explicitly:

Proposed Language

Section 802G. Board of Education Budget — Respective Roles. In the budget process, the respective roles of the Board of Education and the Board of Finance with regard to the Board of Education budget shall be as follows:

(1) The Board of Education shall prepare and submit its itemized budget estimate in accordance with Section 802B of this Charter and Section 10-222 of the Connecticut General Statutes.

(2) The Board of Finance shall review the Board of Education's estimate and may adjust the total appropriation to be recommended for the Board of Education as part of the proposed budget presented to the Annual Town Budget Meeting. In making such adjustments, the Board of Finance shall consider the Board of Education's estimate alongside all other municipal needs and available revenues.

(3) The Board of Finance shall not direct the allocation of funds among specific line items within the Board of Education budget. The money appropriated for the maintenance of public schools shall be expended by and in the discretion of the Board of Education, in accordance with Section 10-222 of the Connecticut General Statutes.

(4) Nothing in this section shall limit the obligation of the Board of Finance to confer with the Board of Education pursuant to Section 802A, to make spending recommendations and suggestions pursuant to Section 10-222 of the Connecticut General Statutes, or to hold public hearings on the budget pursuant to Section 802F of this Charter.

What This Does

  • Consolidates the BOE budget rules in one place instead of requiring readers to piece together 802E, 802F, 803B, and C.G.S. 10-222.
  • Explicitly preserves the collaborative process (conferring, recommendations, hearings) while making the bottom-line authority unambiguous.
  • Paragraph (4) ensures the new section doesn't inadvertently eliminate the recommendation-and-response process required by the 2024 amendment to C.G.S. 10-222.

Suggestion 4: Conforming Change to Section 803B

Current Language

The Town Meeting may not alter any specific items contained in the budget proposed by the local Board of Education.

Proposed Language

The Town Meeting may not alter specific line items within the Board of Education budget. The Town Meeting may reduce the total amount appropriated for the Board of Education, subject to the same limitation that applies to all other portions of the budget: the Town Meeting shall only have the power to reduce or modify, but not increase the budget or any portion thereof.

What This Does

The current 803B language about the BOE is a single sentence that could be read as prohibiting any change to the BOE budget at Town Meeting — including a reduction of the total. This proposed revision makes clear that the Town Meeting's general power to reduce (already stated in the same subsection) applies to the BOE total, while line-item alterations remain prohibited. This aligns with the structural logic: the BOE controls internal allocation; the Town's democratic bodies control the total.

What These Changes Preserve

It's worth emphasizing what this package does not change:

  • The BOE still prepares its own budget. No other body drafts the BOE's estimate.
  • The BOE still controls its line items. How the appropriation is spent internally remains entirely within the BOE's discretion.
  • The collaborative process remains intact. The October conference (802A), the 10-day recommendation window (C.G.S. 10-222), the public hearing (802F), and the joint March review meetings (802D) all continue.
  • The Town Meeting and referendum remain the final authority. The BOF proposes; the voters dispose.
  • The Minimum Budget Requirement (MBR) under state law continues to set a floor below which the BOE appropriation cannot fall, regardless of what the BOF, Town Meeting, or referendum decide.

The changes simply make explicit what is currently implied: that the BOF's role as the municipal fiscal authority includes setting the total BOE appropriation as part of a balanced overall budget, while respecting the BOE's autonomy over how that appropriation is spent.
